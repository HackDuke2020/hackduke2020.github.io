Improved Effectiveness of Active Quarantine and Introduction of Hospitalization

In the last simulation, we attempt to introduce two more factors: active quarantine and hospitalization. In the simulation, quarantine means that these infected individuals can no longer infect other susceptibles. More specifically, we assume no active testing, so people who are asymptomatic or in the latency period (not showing any symptoms) do not get quarantined. For hospitalization, we included the intensive care unit and the number of beds available for every 100 individuals. Thus, the intensive care rate indicates what percentage of the infected population needs intensive care to avoid dying.

Conclusion

In conclusion, following the previous simulations, it is evident that when we introduced the simulated preventive measures, both the final size of the epidemic (number of individuals that have been infected) and the total number of deaths are decreased. Both visually and intuitively, we can observe that these widely adopted measures are indeed effective at mitigating the impact of an epidemic, or a pandemic for that matter.
