Stock Price


This graph shows the Nasdaq stock market data for the last 60 days. Nasdaq is a very good indicator of general U.S. stock market performance, which is in turn an effective measure of America’s economy. The graph is created with the interactive Google chart tool and Thomas Wagenaar’s Stock.js, which scrapes down live market data. This graph and all the graphs below are all live, scraping new data and rendering in the browser in real time.

Covid v. Market


At the beginning of the pandemic in March, the market crashes due to exponentially rising cases of Covid. However, after several legislation concerning relief packages and bailouts are passed, the market soon goes back to normal and begins to dramatically rise. In fact, it’s in its best shape now since the 1990s. Basically, when ordinary people are getting evicted and losing their jobs, big companies are doing more than just fine. As shown in this graph, the market fluctuations are hardly related to the monotonic increase in Covid cases now.
